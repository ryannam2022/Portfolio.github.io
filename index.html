ABOUT ME

Hello, my name is Ryanna Mowatt. I am a Junior honors Criminology major from Orlando, Florida. On campus, I am involved in many organizations, each of them continuing different passions I have in my personal life. I am the Vice President of the College of Arts and Sciences Council which allows me to lead my fellow COAS students in a way that provides them with informational programming, community building opportunities, and an student leader that advocates for their needs. I am also a Spring 2025 initiate of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated, Alpha Chapter, an international organization that is committed to scholarship, sisterhood, and service to all mankind. I am also a Policy Intern for the DC Council for Court Excellence, where I conduct research on justice-based policies in DC, aiming to make DC justice systems more equitable. I am in this Data Sceince class this semester to learn more about coding to do better in my research. After graduating from Howard in 2027, I have plans to go to graduate school to get a JD/PhD in Public Policy. Being enrolled in this class has taught me a lot about the value of coding, and I am able to apply the things I have learned in my research and statistics courses as well. Having an understanding of Data Science will also make me a uniquely equipped candidate when I apply for graduate school.
